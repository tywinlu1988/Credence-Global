Engine predictions are benchmarked against the actual credit status determined by an expert committee post-hoc. Note: due to the extremely small sample size, all statistical metrics should be treated as preliminary references only and lack statistical significance.

Calibration Analysis Conclusions

The current engine shows good calibration in the high-confidence range (>70%). The calibration error in the 90-100% range is only +5% (actual 100% vs predicted 95%), which is practically acceptable. The 70-89% range is slightly conservative (actual 100% vs predicted 78%), meaning the engine underestimates its own accuracy in high-confidence judgments.

In the 30-49% medium-confidence range, there is notable systematic overestimation (actual risk rate 0% vs predicted 42%), with a calibration error of -42%. Although this interval contains only 1 case, this bias pattern warrants attention -- the engine tends to overestimate risk when it is "less certain," which could lead to systematically lower ratings for medium-risk entities.

It is recommended to add Platt Scaling or Isotonic Regression for post-calibration in the next version, especially targeted at the 30-49% confidence interval.

Statistical Limitations Statement

The following lists the main statistical limitations of this validation report. Any decision made using the conclusions of this report must be based on a full understanding and consideration of these limitations.

1. Sample Size Limitation

The current validation set contains only 8 cases, far too few to draw statistically robust inferences about engine performance. All accuracy/recall metrics have wide confidence intervals. For example, FNR=0% in an 8-sample set has a 95% confidence interval of approximately 0% to 34% (Clopper-Pearson exact method), meaning the true miss rate could be as high as 34%. A target sample size of at least 100 cases is recommended to obtain meaningful statistical inference.

2. Selection Bias

The current validation cases were manually selected by engineers as "typical" cases, not through a true random sampling procedure. The selection process may have unconsciously biased toward cases where the engine performs well, or toward large-cap blue-chip companies more familiar to analysts. Stratified random sampling by industry, rating tier, and credit cycle phase is recommended for future rounds.

3. Insufficient Industry Coverage

Only 3 industries are covered, and all samples are investment-grade (BBB and above). The engine's discriminatory ability for high-yield (BB and below) entities has not been validated -- this is precisely where the core value of a credit analysis engine lies. Furthermore, industry-specific risk factors (such as Real Estate pre-sale fund supervision, Financial regulatory capital constraints) have not been tested in the validation.

4. Time Horizon Limitation

The validation window covers only the past 12 months of data and does not span the full credit cycle (especially the downturn and crisis phases). The Chinese credit market was in a relatively stable period from 2025Q2 to 2026Q2, with default rates at historical lows. The engine's predictive performance in adverse environments (e.g., the 2020 pandemic, the 2022 real estate crisis) has not been tested. It is recommended to build a validation set covering at least one full credit cycle (5-7 years).

5. Backtesting Overfitting Risk

This validation uses backtesting -- validating engine predictions against historical data. Backtesting carries an inherent risk of overfitting, as engine parameters may have been implicitly adjusted during development to "fit" the historical data. Recommendations: (1) establish a separate Out-of-Sample validation set, locked before engine development begins and not used during development; (2) implement k-fold cross-validation (k=5 or 10) to assess stability; (3) introduce time-series Walk-Forward Validation to ensure training data points precede validation data.

6. Sample Imbalance in Rating Migration

The distribution of 2 upgrades and 0 downgrades in the validation sample may reflect the specific credit cycle characteristics of the validation period rather than the engine's true prediction preference. When the validation period enters a credit downturn, the proportion of downgrade cases will increase significantly, requiring a reassessment of the engine's predictive performance in a declining market.

Important Disclaimer: This validation report is for internal R&D evaluation only. Any investment decisions or credit extensions based on this report are made at your own risk. The engine is currently in alpha stage and has not been independently verified by a third party. It should not be used as the sole or primary basis for credit decisions until the following validation milestones are met: (1) independent sample size ≥ 100; (2) coverage of ≥ 10 industries; (3) inclusion of a full credit cycle downturn phase; (4) 97.5% confidence interval upper bound for FNR ≤ 10%; (5) independent third-party audit.
