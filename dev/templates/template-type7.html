Why Traditional Financial Analysis Fails in This Industry

Factors Rendering Traditional Analysis Ineffective

  • Capacity ≠ Output ≠ Sales: PV companies are asset-heavy with large capacities, but capacity utilization fluctuates wildly; balance sheets cannot reflect actual output efficiency
  • Income Statement Lag of 1-2 Years: Long-term contract orders and inventory cycles cause inter-period mismatches; current period profits may reflect operating conditions from six months ago
  • Technology Roadmap Disruption Risk: P-type → N-type → HBC → Perovskite, technology iteration cycles of 2-3 years; existing assets may be rapidly obsoleted
  • Policy-Driven Nonlinear Shocks: Policy impacts such as subsidy phase-outs, anti-dumping tariffs, and UFLPA cannot be modeled through historical data
  • Hidden Off-Balance Sheet Liabilities: Off-balance sheet risks from long-term contract purchase commitments, equipment finance leases, and overseas subsidiary guarantees are substantial

Most Significant Factors (Weight Ranking)

  1. Capacity Utilization (Weight 15%) — Survival line under supply glut
  2. Technology Roadmap Selection (Weight 12%) — Determines 3-5 year competitiveness
  3. Unit Cost Curve (Weight 11%) — Profit/loss baseline in price wars
  4. Overseas Revenue Share (Weight 9%) — Geopolitical risk exposure
  5. R&D Conversion Efficiency (Weight 8%) — Patent → Product → Revenue
  6. Cash Flow Self-Sufficiency (Weight 7%) — Capex endurance
  7. Policy Dependency (Weight 6%) — Subsidies/Tariffs/Carbon Tariffs
  8. Inventory Health (Weight 5%) — Write-down risk
  9. Supplier Concentration (Weight 4%) — Polysilicon supply security
  10. ESG Compliance Cost (Weight 3%) — Carbon border tax

Methodology Limitations Statement

# Limitation Description Impact Level Mitigation Measures
1 The model assumes industry cyclicality is predictable, but geopolitical shocks (e.g., UFLPA) may cause nonlinear regime shifts High Introducing scenario analysis module covering extreme geopolitical scenarios
2 Financial indicator thresholds are based on historical data regression and may not accurately reflect sudden changes during technology roadmap transitions Medium Quarterly threshold updates, incorporating technology roadmap transition probability factors
3 ESG assessment relies on voluntarily disclosed data, subject to selective disclosure bias Medium Supplementing third-party ESG data sources, using mandatory disclosure data for cross-checking
4 Pyramid weights are static and do not fully reflect dynamic changes with the industry cycle Medium Planning to introduce dynamic weight mechanism in v3.0, automatically adjusting based on industry cycle phase
5 Sample bias: validation data primarily from leading listed companies; discrimination for unlisted SMEs not fully verified Medium Unlisted company sample collection plan launched, expected 2027Q1 completion of expansion
6 Veto conditions are hard rules that may produce false positives in special circumstances Low Analyst Committee waiver authority retained; each waiver case must be documented
