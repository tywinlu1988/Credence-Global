9.3 Methodological Limitation Statement

Limitation 1: Signal Quality Dependency

The calculation quality of the SRI depends on the accuracy of the input Track-A ratings and Track-B signals. If these underlying signals themselves have biases (e.g., inflated ratings), the SRI will also be distorted. References the engine's "pseudo-high rating" identification mechanism as auxiliary verification of SRI quality.

Limitation 2: Lag Behind Exogenous Shocks

The SRI framework is based on industry fundamental signals and cannot provide early warning for exogenous, non-credit shocks (e.g., pandemics, natural disasters, geopolitical conflicts). For known external risks (e.g., trade frictions, regulatory policy changes), these can be reflected in advance through the outlook assessment, partially mitigating the lag.

Limitation 3: Incomplete Industry Coverage

The framework aggregates at the level of 19 GICS-based industries; intra-industry divergence (e.g., IG vs HY issuers within one sector) is averaged out. Pair SRI with single-issuer Track A analysis for issuer-level differentiation.

Limitation 4: Parameter Subjectivity

The industry risk score thresholds (3.0/5.0/6.0), penalty factors (0.5 points), and thermometer thresholds (0.5/1.0/1.8) are all based on subjective judgment and historical calibration, not statistical optimization.
