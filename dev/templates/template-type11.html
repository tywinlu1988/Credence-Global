Second-Order Effect Analysis

The transmission of stress scenarios is not limited to first-order financial indicators. The following secondary transmission effects may further amplify credit shocks. Second-order effects represent "stress within stress," and their quantification requires consideration of industry structural characteristics and the company's own supply chain and financing ecosystem.

Inventory Write-Down Spiral

In a down cycle for PV module prices, inventory write-down risk exhibits a self-reinforcing trend. The Severe scenario assumes module prices fall another 15% in 2026, requiring impairment provisions of approximately RMB 2.2-2.8 billion on cost-basis inventories, further eroding profits and net capital. More critically, inventory write-downs may trigger creditors to revalue collateral, potentially leading to demands for additional guarantees or early repayment.

Potential Impairment: 2.2-2.8 bn Triggers Negative Profit Collateral Revaluation Risk

Approximately 65% of LONGi's inventory consists of finished goods and goods in transit. For every 1% decline in module prices, inventory impairment is approximately RMB 150-180 million. LONGi's inventory turnover days are 78, and whether price decline risk can be released through sales in the short term is uncertain.

Working Capital Freeze

Downstream customers' willingness to delay payments increases during industry downturns. Accounts receivable turnover days extend to 125 days (+45 days) under the Severe scenario, combined with inventory turnover slowing to 140 days, tying up additional working capital of approximately RMB 5.5-7.0 billion. Some banks may freeze credit lines upon seeing deteriorating liquidity indicators (assuming a 50% reduction in credit availability under the Severe scenario).

Funds Tied Up: 5.5-7.0 bn Credit Freeze Risk Upstream/Downstream Payment Term Squeeze

Approximately 35% of downstream customers are small-to-medium EPC contractors, whose credit quality typically deteriorates first during economic downturns. It is recommended to establish a customer credit rating management system and maintain asset monetization tools such as receivables ABS.
