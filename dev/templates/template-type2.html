IV. Key Findings & Risk Warnings

Finding 1 · Financial Vulnerability Cliff

Yidao's asset-liability ratio of 92%, current ratio of 0.65x, and operating cash flow net outflow of 0.85 billion RMB — all three indicators have hit the framework's red thresholds. In the L3 Financial Strength tier (weight 35%), the score gap with LONGi reaches 6.6 points, contributing 42% of the total variance. The core of financial vulnerability is not the deterioration of a single indicator, but the simultaneous collapse of four dimensions — solvency, liquidity, profitability, and cash flow — forming a "financial death spiral": declining profitability leads to cash flow depletion, forcing increased debt to sustain operations, while high debt in turn erodes profit margins.

Finding 2 · Matthew Effect in Industry Position Accelerating

During the industry consolidation period, LONGi as the global leader, by leveraging its BC cell technology and vertical integration cost advantage, continues to expand market share (18% in 2025). Yidao lacks both scale effects (market share below 2%) and differentiated technology reserves (TOPCon route offers no advantage), facing elimination risk. From 2024-2026, 12 tail-end solar companies have already exited the market; Yidao is likely the next. Increasing industry concentration will further widen the gap.

Finding 3 · Governance Defects as Hidden Risk Amplifier

Yidao's controlling shareholder holds 72% equity with frequent management changes, scoring only 1.5 on governance. The risk of related-party transactions (purchases/sales/fund borrowing possibly conducted with the controlling shareholder's other entities) due to one-share dominance, and strategy defocus (technology roadmap oscillating between TOPCon and HJT), will amplify operational risks during market downturns. Although governance accounts for only 20% by weight, it is the only tier that can "transmit and amplify" risks from other tiers and should be given higher attention.

Finding 4 · External Support Divergence Will Accelerate Credit Divergence

LONGi has 82 billion RMB in bank credit facilities and smooth capital market access (successfully issued 5 billion green convertible bonds in 2025); Yidao's credit is nearly exhausted (3.5 billion utilized out of 3.8 billion total) and its IPO is blocked. In a tightening liquidity environment, the divergence in external support could become the trigger for Yidao's default: once banks call in loans or fail to renew credit, it will directly trigger a liquidity crisis and cross-default.

Finding 5 · Framework Weights Reasonable but Optimizable in Current Cycle

The current framework's L3 Financial Strength (35%) + L2 Industry Operations (20%) totals 55%, which is broadly reasonable in a downturn. However, based on contribution data, L3 actually contributes 42% of the variance but only carries 35% weight; L2 contributes 22% but only carries 20% weight. It is recommended to temporarily increase L2+L3 combined weight to 65-70% during the consolidation period, and reduce L1 macro environment weight from 10% to 5%, to more sensitively capture credit divergence during the industry shakeout.
