Scan Summary: LONGi Green Energy ESG composite score 6.5/10, in the upper-middle tier of the photovoltaic industry. The environmental dimension stands out (8.2 points), driven by active carbon management and green product strategy; the social dimension is solid (6.5 points), but the proportion of dispatched workers is high and warrants attention; the governance dimension is weak (4.8 points), with board independence and minority shareholder protection mechanisms needing improvement. Overall fraud/evasion risk is low, but related-party transaction pricing fairness and auditor tenure require ongoing monitoring.

Environmental Dimension

The environmental dimension carries a 35% weight in the overall ESG score. The assessment covers five sub-items: carbon management, pollutant emissions and compliance, green transition, resource utilization, and biodiversity. LONGi's environmental performance ranks in the top 10% of the photovoltaic industry.

Carbon Management

LONGi has achieved a 48% reduction in operational carbon emissions from peak levels by 2025, reaching its interim targets ahead of schedule. Scope 1+2 emissions total approximately 1.52 million tCO₂e, with carbon intensity per unit revenue down 12% year-over-year. The 2025 CDP climate rating is "A-" (Leadership level), the highest in the photovoltaic industry.

Positive Signal Impact: Low Risk

Green electricity usage has reached 65%, with distributed PV projects under construction expected to cover all production bases by 2027. A 2030 carbon neutrality roadmap study has also been initiated.

Environmental Penalties & Compliance

No major environmental penalties in the past 12 months (fines exceeding RMB 100,000 or media coverage). 2 general environmental rectification notices (online COD monitoring anomaly for wastewater, non-standard temporary solid waste storage), both fully remediated and verified by the local ecological environment bureau. Cumulative environmental capex of RMB 1.2 billion, operating costs of RMB 0.8 billion.

Neutral-to-Positive Signal Impact: Low Risk

Environmental investment budget increased 18% YoY in 2026, focusing on hazardous waste reduction and zero-emission campus construction. The company has committed to completing zero-wastewater-discharge retrofits at all production bases by 2027.

Green Transition & Product Carbon Footprint

The Hi-MO 7 series module has a product carbon footprint 28% lower than the industry average (based on EPD certification). LONGi has joined RE100, EP100, and EV100, committing to 100% renewable energy for global operations by 2028. The hydrogen business (LONGi Hydrogen) has an electrolyzer capacity of 2.5 GW, providing a second growth curve for the green transition.

Positive Signal Impact: Credit Enhancing

Approximately 15% of annual R&D spending is allocated to green technology development, including low-energy monocrystalline growth technology, fluorine-free packaging materials, and PV module recycling technology.

Water Resource Management

Production water recycling rate is 78%, with water consumption per unit product down 32% from 2020. Production bases in water-stressed regions such as Ningxia and Yunnan have implemented water-saving technology upgrades. Monocrystalline wafer unit water consumption is 0.12 tons/wafer, better than the industry average of 0.18 tons/wafer.

Low Risk

Waste & Resource Recycling

General solid waste comprehensive utilization rate is 92%, hazardous waste compliant disposal rate is 100%. A pilot PV module recycling project has been established with an annual processing capacity of 5,000 tons. The first commercial PV module recycling plant is expected by 2027.

Social Dimension

The social dimension carries a 30% weight in the overall ESG score. The assessment covers five sub-items: workplace safety, labor management, product quality, community relations, and supply chain social responsibility. LONGi's social dimension performance is above average, but labor management presents potential compliance and reputational risks.

Workplace Safety

Two minor injury incidents occurred in the past 12 months (both mechanical injuries during equipment maintenance), with no serious/fatal accidents. The Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR) is 0.38, better than the photovoltaic industry benchmark (0.52). However, this has increased from the prior year (0.31), warranting attention. An AI visual safety inspection system has been deployed across six major production bases.

Neutral-to-Positive Signal Impact: Low Risk

Safety training coverage and completion rates are both 100%, and the three-level safety education implementation rate for new employees is 100%.

Labor Management & Employment Compliance

The 2025 annual report shows the proportion of dispatched labor is approximately 9.5%, close to the regulatory cap of 10%. Social media has seen complaints about "excessive overtime for operators during peak seasons." Although not constituting an administrative sanction, this could trigger social responsibility controversy and brand reputational risk. Employee turnover is approximately 15%, with front-line operator turnover around 22%.

Negative Signal Impact: Medium Risk

The company has committed to controlling the dispatched labor ratio within 8% by 2026, and plans to raise the minimum hourly wage for front-line workers to at least 120% of the local minimum standard.

Product Quality & Customer Satisfaction

PV module return rate is below 0.3%, with warranty-period complaint rate around 0.8%. The company has passed the ISO 9001:2025 certification upgrade audit. The LONGi brand has received PV Magazine's bankability rating "A" for 8 consecutive years. Zero product recalls and zero quality lawsuits in 2025.

Positive Signal Impact: Low Risk

Approximately 8% of R&D spending is dedicated to online product quality inspection technology, with 6 AI visual defect detection production lines built.

Community Relations & Social Welfare

Community welfare investment in 2025 was approximately RMB 32 million, mainly focused on PV poverty alleviation, rural education revitalization, and disaster relief projects. Distributed PV systems have been installed for over 20,000 rural households. No major community conflicts or complaints have occurred.

Governance Dimension

The governance dimension carries a 35% weight in the overall ESG score. The assessment covers five sub-items: ownership structure, board effectiveness, information disclosure, minority shareholder protection, and compliance & anti-corruption. LONGi's governance dimension is weak and is the main drag on the overall ESG score. The following analysis is linked to the governance-fraud-risk module.

Ownership Structure & Control

The ultimate controllers, Li Zhenguo and Li Xiyan, together hold approximately 18.2%, and the top ten shareholders collectively hold about 38%. Ownership concentration is at a moderate level within the photovoltaic industry. No margin call risk from share pledges; the pledge ratio is about 1.3%. However, there is significant overlap between non-independent directors and management on the board, raising concerns about "management-dominated boards."

Neutral Signal Impact: Low Risk

Institutional investor shareholding is approximately 22%, of which foreign institutions hold about 8% through the Shanghai-Hong Kong and Shenzhen-Hong Kong Stock Connects.

Board Structure & Effectiveness

The board has 11 seats: 4 independent directors (36%), slightly below the conventional expectation for domestic listed companies (>=1/3). 1 female director (9%). The audit committee and compensation committee are both chaired by independent directors, but the nomination committee is chaired by the board chair, raising independence concerns. Board meeting attendance is 98.5%, but independent directors have zero records of voting against or abstaining on management proposals, raising concerns about substantive independence.

Negative Signal Impact: Medium Risk

The ESG committee under the board held 2 meetings in 2025, with discussion topics mainly focused on compliance reporting; depth of strategic engagement is insufficient.

Information Disclosure Transparency

ESG reports have been published for 5 consecutive years, but some granular data (such as plant-level emission data, supply chain labor audit reports) are not publicly available. The 2025 Shanghai Stock Exchange disclosure rating is B (Good), down from A in the prior year. This is mainly attributed to issues with timeliness and completeness of related-party transaction disclosures.

Neutral-to-Negative Signal Impact: Medium Risk

The financial report received an unqualified audit opinion from PricewaterhouseCoopers, but the number of key audit matters increased from 2 to 3 year-over-year, with a new item on "provision for inventory write-downs."

Minority Shareholder Protection Mechanism

Cumulative voting is not explicitly stipulated in the articles of association. Minority shareholder attendance at the 2025 general meeting was only about 3.2%. In the past three years, there have been 2 joint proposals from minority shareholders (regarding dividend ratios and H-share issuance pricing), both rejected by the board. The dividend payout ratio is maintained at a relatively low 30%, below the photovoltaic industry average of 40%. Linked detection with the governance-fraud-risk module shows: related-party transaction risk score of 7.3/10 (moderately high), mainly concerning the pricing fairness of silicon material purchases from associates.

Negative Signal Impact: Elevated Risk

Recommendation: Monitor whether the 2026 general meeting will amend the articles to introduce cumulative voting — this is a key window to observe management's attitude.

Compliance & Anti-Corruption

An anti-bribery compliance management system has been established, certified under ISO 37001. In 2025, there were no commercial bribery, unfair competition, or antitrust lawsuits or penalties. Employee anti-corruption training coverage is 100%. The whistleblowing hotline received 12 reports throughout the year, all investigated and responded to.

Low Risk

Related-Party Transaction Management

Total related-party transactions in 2025 were approximately RMB 3.8 billion (2.8% of revenue), of which purchases of silicon material from associates accounted for about RMB 2.2 billion. Related-party transaction pricing uses a "market price reference + cost-plus" method, but the specific markup ratio is not disclosed. Independent directors issued an affirmative opinion on the related-party transactions, but no pricing reasonableness analysis report has been made public.
