# Industry Pyramid Specifications

## 1. Solar/PV + Energy Storage (Policy-Driven)

| Layer | Weight | Key Metrics | One-Shot Veto |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1 Policy/Macro | 35% | Policy direction × technology roadmap alignment; subsidy phaseout timing; export tax rebate exposure; curtailment/grid connection policy | Core product explicitly restricted/eliminated by policy |
| L2 Technology | 30% | Technology roadmap correctness; efficiency gap vs leader; patent portfolio; SOE bidding inclusion; capacity utilization | PERC capacity >70% |
| L3 Supply Chain | 20% | Vertical integration level; polysilicon long-term contract coverage; overseas capacity; customer concentration; price sensitivity coefficient | Single customer >50% AND customer has own risk |
| L4 Financial | 15% | Gross margin trend; debt ratio; cash/short-term debt; operating cash flow direction; financing capability | Negative operating cash flow 3+ consecutive years AND cash runway <6 months |

**Validated:** Longi (7.00/BBB+) vs Yidao (1.50/CCC) — framework distinguished with 5.5-point gap

## 2. Semiconductor/IC (Policy-Driven) — 5 Layers

| Layer | Weight | Key Metrics | One-Shot Veto |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1 Geopolitics | 30-35% | Entity List/SDN exposure; equipment supply chain security (ASML/AMAT/Lam dependency); EDA tool licensing; foundry dependency; IP authorization restrictions | On Entity List or SDN List |
| L2 Technology | 25-30% | Technology node positioning; roadmap credibility (tape-out success rate); IP portfolio quality; R&D conversion efficiency; talent depth; technology disruption resilience (Chiplet/RISC-V/silicon photonics) | — |
| L3 Market Position | 15-20% | Market share trajectory; end-customer quality; design-win pipeline; ASP/gross margin trends; substitution threats | — |
| L4 Policy/Capital | 10-15% | National IC Fund investment status; local government support quality; STAR Board/IPO feasibility; subsidy sustainability; standard-setting participation | — |
| L5 Financial | 5-10% | Cash runway; financing rhythm; receivables quality; goodwill/total assets; related-party transactions; controlling shareholder pledge ratio | Goodwill/total assets >20% + interest coverage <2x |

**Validated:** Tsinghua Unigroup — L5+L4 both red at T-17 months. All signals in 2018 annual report.

**Note:** L5 weight should be increased to 15-20% for M&A-driven enterprises with high goodwill.

## 3. High-End Equipment/Machine Tools (Tech-Barrier)

| Layer | Weight | Key Metrics | One-Shot Veto |
|---|---|---|---|
| L1 Policy | 20% | Import substitution policy coverage; "First Set" insurance/directory inclusion; R&D super-deduction eligibility | — |
| L2 Technology Autonomy | 35% | Core component self-sufficiency rate (CNC system/servo/spindle); precision pyramid position; R&D conversion efficiency; technology reserve depth; maintenance service capability | CNC system + spindle + servo ALL outsourced, no domestic substitute |
| L3 Operations | 25% | Order visibility (backlog coverage months); customer structure health; delivery/receivables cycle; capacity utilization | — |
| L4 Financial | 20% | Adjusted gross margin (after R&D capitalization correction); true cash flow coverage; true leverage; receivables/inventory quality | Five financial traps must be corrected before analysis |

**Five Financial Traps specific to this industry:**
1. Revenue recognition timing distortion (Q4 concentration >40%)
2. R&D capitalization manipulation (>40% capitalization rate)
3. Inventory realizability (custom WIP may have zero salvage value)
4. Retention/tail payment hidden bad debts
5. Related-party transaction pricing manipulation

**Key distinction:** Biotech (pre-revenue) and Pharma (commercialized) require completely different financial analysis frameworks.

## 6. New Energy Vehicles (Consolidation) — Dual Track Required

**NEV analysis MUST use separate frameworks for OEMs vs supply chain companies.**

### OEM Track (Survival Probability)

| Layer | Weight | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| L1 Survival Position | 25% | Monthly sales volume & trend; 5-factor survival probability model (sales 30% + utilization 20% + cash 25% + brand 15% + tech iteration 10%) |
| L2 Technology | 20% | Smart driving capability; powertrain efficiency; vehicle architecture; OTA capability |
| L3 Operations | 20% | Capacity utilization; unit economics (per-vehicle gross/net profit); inventory digestion rate |
| L4 Financial | 15% | Net cash burn rate; cash runway (months); gross margin trend; hidden liabilities (dealer rebates, warranty provisions) |
| L5 Policy/Export | 10% | Subsidy dependency; dual-credit exposure; overseas market progress; tariff/certification barriers |

### Supply Chain Track (Profit Fortress)

| Layer | Weight | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| L1 Profit Fortress | 30% | Gross margin stability; decoupling rate (sector margin change / vehicle ASP change); ROIC; certification barriers (IATF16949 + customer nomination) |
| L2 Technology | 20% | Technology roadmap resilience; customer switching cost; production yield rate; automation level |
| L3 Client Quality | 22% | Multi-dimensional binding score (client quality 40% + supply share 25% + contract duration 20% + substitution cost 15%) |
| L4 Financial | 17% | Margin stability coefficient; adjusted receivables turnover; price-in elasticity test |
| L5 Policy/Export | 8% | Overseas capacity; tariff exposure; CBAM/carbon tariff impact |
